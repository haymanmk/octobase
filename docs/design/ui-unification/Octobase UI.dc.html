How memory consolidates during sleep

Maria Konnikovanature.com2 highlights

Each night, the sleeping brain performs a quiet act of curation. Newly encoded memories remain labile for hours after learning, and it is during slow-wave sleep that the hippocampus replays them to the cortex.

This replay is not a passive echo. Spindle density in stage 2 sleep predicts overnight retention — the brain appears to select which traces deserve a permanent home.

Spindles act as gating events: bursts during stage 2 sleep correlate with which memories get replayed, and spindle density predicts overnight retention.
